Direction captured

Introduction / Overview

This PRD defines an iPhone app for holistic women's health, with a primary focus on inside-out acne care, female hormone health, macronutrient visibility, supplement education, lifestyle support for stress and anxiety, exercise logging, adaptive naturopathic-style consult support, and reusable food macro tracking. The product should feel like a functional medicine consultant in the user's pocket while remaining clear that it is a wellness and education tool, not a replacement for licensed medical care.

Problem

College-age women, women in their early 20s, and reproductive-age adults trying to improve acne, hormone health, stress, digestion, energy, and cycle-related symptoms often track macros, supplements, symptoms, lifestyle routines, and exercise across disconnected tools.

Audience

Women who want holistic, functional medicine-informed self-guidance around inside-out acne care, hormones, nutrition, skin, supplements, stress, and daily routines.

MVP Wedge

A four-tab app structure: Lifestyle, Supplements, Macro, and Exercise, with a gear-accessed Settings area for API keys, model selection, API cost tracking, privacy, and data controls.

Clarification Status

This section separates what has been incorporated into the PRD from what still needs a clearer product decision before high-fidelity design or implementation.

Resolved and Incorporated

  • Primary focus is inside-out acne care with hormone support and functional wellness included.
  • Primary choices and guardrails have been incorporated, with all relevant option sets treated as important app inputs or boundaries.
  • Success is centered on useful insight and helping users understand body patterns.
  • Initial audience is college-age women, women in their early 20s, and reproductive-age adults.
  • Bottom tabs are Lifestyle, Supplements, Macro, and Exercise; Settings is reached from a gear icon.
  • Macro tracking is protein-forward and macro-centered. Calories may appear as secondary food data, but calorie deficit and weight-loss strategy are not part of the food module.
  • Macro data uses an editable local JSON library with optional iCloud sync and JSON import/upload.
  • Meal photo analysis supports multi-item meals and restaurant plates after user confirmation.
  • Food data module should adapt the Health-Tracker food structure: Add Food, Log Meal, Food Log, Daily Totals, and Visualize Food.
  • Food module should exclude Better Choices, dynamic burn rate, burn and weight, and how-are-you-feeling sections.
  • Meal planning is deferred out of MVP and should be revisited later.
  • Barcode scanning and external food databases are not needed for MVP.
  • Gemini photo analysis should add confirmed foods/meals into the user's JSON library so repeated foods can be searched and logged without another image analysis call.
  • Reference citations should be included when specific sources are available, especially for non-drug acne best practices and holistic wellness guidance.
  • The app is for one primary user first; a future public version could be sold as a one-time purchase if it proves valuable.
  • Local phone storage and optional iCloud storage are acceptable for the first version.
  • Serving presets are 25%, 50%, 75%, exact amount, 150%, and double, with room for a custom amount.
  • Health assessments are manually initiated at whatever frequency the user wants.
  • Acne flare photo review is in scope with no default image storage.
  • Gemini API key, model selection, and API cost tracking live in Settings.
  • Exercise uses low/medium/high intensity plus duration, with sprint activity choices.
  • Exercise duration sliders use a default range from 5 to 60 minutes in 5-minute increments.
  • Reference UI screenshots have been incorporated for food logging, saved-food search, portion sizing, meal photo capture, exercise duration sliders, acne photo review, and manual health assessment creation.
  • The Ristoro Italian Art Deco design guide is the working app visual direction for SwiftUI styling, mockups, typography, color, controls, framed cards, and tone.
  • Data is local-first with optional iCloud sync.

Remaining Refinement

  • No open product-scope blocker remains in the PRD.
  • High-fidelity app screens should refine the Ristoro-inspired direction in SwiftUI before final implementation polish.

User responses incorporated

Resolved Product Decisions

  • Primary product emphasis is inside-out acne care, while still including the hormone support hub and functional wellness plan concepts.
  • Initial persona is college-age women, women in their early 20s, and reproductive-age adults.
  • Bottom tabs are Lifestyle, Supplements, Macro, and Exercise. Settings is opened from a gear icon and includes API setup, API storage, model selection, and cost tracking.
  • Acne is the most important MVP concern, but PMS, irregular cycles, PCOS support, bloating, cravings, fatigue, mood, cycle awareness, and related hormone-health concerns remain in scope.
  • The app should support acne flare photo review with Gemini, follow-up questions, and holistic non-drug wellness suggestions. Acne images should not be stored by default; the assessment, context, and recommendations may be saved for trend and follow-up use.
  • The Macro tab is protein-forward and macro-centered, with protein, carbohydrates, and fat as the primary fields. Calories can be retained as secondary food information, but the app must not frame the experience around calorie deficit, weight loss, burn rate, or restriction.
  • The macro library should be an actual editable JSON-backed local store with iCloud sync as an option, plus import/upload support for an existing saved JSON database.
  • Confirmed food photo results and confirmed Gemini estimates should become reusable JSON library records, creating a custom library of the user's most frequently eaten foods over time.
  • Meal photo analysis should support multi-item meals and restaurant plates, not only single foods.
  • Barcode scanning and external food database integrations are out of MVP; the preferred approach is photo/upload/typed entry plus the user's growing custom JSON library.
  • Meal planning is deferred out of MVP and can be added later.
  • Visualizations should include daily macro rings, weekly macro bars, macro-to-symptom comparisons, supplement adherence, stress trends, acne trends, and cycle overlays.
  • The naturopathic consult should ask adaptive questions one at a time, up to 10 questions, and generate a practitioner-style report with cited references where available.
  • Supplements guidance should connect possible nutrient areas to the user's goals, such as acne and protein needs, and can include supplement suggestions, clinician questions, and areas to investigate with safety boundaries.
  • Lifestyle practices should react to the user's logged data, goals, and consult responses rather than relying on static generic recommendations.
  • Exercise logging should use low, medium, and high intensity labels for weightlifting and sprint bursts, with sprint activity choices such as running, bike, rowing, and boxing.
  • Exercise duration sliders should run from 5 to 60 minutes in 5-minute increments for both weightlifting and sprint/burst sessions.
  • The app is initially personal-use oriented and may later be sold as a one-time purchase.
  • Data should live locally on-device first, with optional iCloud sync.
  • Safety posture should be lightweight because this is a custom app for one knowledgeable user, but the product should still avoid diagnosis, drug guidance, or medical treatment claims.
  • Gemini model selection should be exposed to users because they provide their own API key.
  • The food log UI should adapt the reference structure: Add Food connected to a JSON food library, New Food, Log Meal with camera/photo upload, Food Log rows with serving size and macro details, Daily Totals, and Visualize Food. Calories may remain as secondary row/totals data, but deficit and calorie burn are intentionally excluded.
  • Saved foods should support serving amount chips for 25%, 50%, 75%, exact amount, 150%, and double, plus a custom amount and Cancel/Log actions before a food is added to the daily macro log.
  • Exercise UI should use activity or intensity cards plus visible duration sliders for weightlifting and sprint/burst sessions.
  • The acne workflow should be promoted as a first-priority screen: photo/upload, consent, follow-up questions, holistic non-drug suggestions, saved assessment, and no default image storage.
  • The PRD should include a manually initiated health assessment creation flow that pulls from macros, eating patterns, active treatments, holistic treatments, acne photo findings, supplements, lifestyle, exercise, symptoms, and consult answers.

Goals

Finalized product goals incorporated from approved product decisions.

  1. Help users track daily food, meals, protein, carbohydrates, fat, and secondary calorie data in the same place they track hormone and skin-related signals, without centering the experience on calorie counting or calorie deficit.
  2. Provide a naturopathic and functional medicine-inspired consult area that asks adaptive one-at-a-time questions, summarizes patterns, cites references where available, and suggests wellness considerations with appropriate safety boundaries.
  3. Support supplement tracking, including schedule, dosage notes, adherence, and user-observed effects.
  4. Suggest possible vitamin, mineral, and supplement areas to discuss based on user goals, feelings, acne patterns, protein needs, and logged signals while avoiding diagnosis.
  5. Recommend data-reactive lifestyle, meditative, and therapeutic practices that encourage mindfulness, slowing down, nervous system regulation, stress relief, and anxiety reduction.
  6. Provide an exercise tab where users can log weightlifting and sprint-burst activity by selecting activity, low/medium/high intensity, and duration instead of entering individual exercises.
  7. Create an inside-out acne care experience that connects nutrition, hormone health, stress, lifestyle, supplements, flare photo review, and saved recommendation history without shame-based or medical-claim language.
  8. Define a focused Swift iPhone MVP that can be implemented with clear privacy, safety, and AI model-selection requirements.
  9. Support a user-provided Gemini API key, user-visible model selection, and API cost tracking across daily, weekly, average, and total usage.
  10. Give users a clear manual health assessment workflow that turns logged data into an editable wellness summary and current focus areas.

User Stories

Implementation-ready story areas for the current PRD.

16 draft story areas

US-001

Women's Health Onboarding

As a new user, I want to define my hormone, skin, nutrition, supplement, and stress priorities so that the app can tailor my first daily tracking plan.

  • Onboarding captures goals across hormone health, acne or skin concerns, cycle context, nutrition, supplements, stress, sleep, and mindfulness.
  • Onboarding makes clear that the app provides wellness education and tracking, not diagnosis or medical treatment.
  • Users can skip optional questions and update answers later.

US-002

Macro-First Food Tracking

As a user, I want to type what I ate and get macronutrients from my saved food library or Gemini so that logging gets faster over time.

  • Users can add breakfast, lunch, dinner, snacks, drinks, and notes for each day by typing a natural-language description of what they ate.
  • The app first checks the user's local JSON-backed food macro library for known foods and meals before calling Gemini.
  • The food log shows each saved item with timestamp, serving description, optional secondary calories, protein, carbohydrates, fat, and a remove action.
  • When Gemini estimates macros for a new food or meal, the user must review and confirm the estimate before it is added to the daily log or saved library.
  • The app shows daily totals for protein, carbohydrates, fat, and secondary calories, with extra visibility for protein intake and no required macro targets in MVP.
  • Calories can appear in the food module, but calorie deficit, burn rate, weight-loss countdowns, and restriction framing are excluded.

US-003

Photo-Based Macro Capture

As a user, I want to photograph or upload a meal image and confirm the identified foods and macros so that the app can log the meal and improve my saved macro library.

  • Users can take a photo or upload an existing photo from the iPhone photo library.
  • Gemini analyzes the image and returns structured food items, estimated serving descriptions, and protein, carbohydrate, and fat values.
  • The user can edit food names, portions, and macro values before accepting the result.
  • Only user-confirmed results are added to the daily log and saved to the JSON-backed food macro library.
  • Previously confirmed foods and meals are reused for future search and logging to reduce unnecessary Gemini calls and image analysis.
  • Over time, the user's most frequently eaten foods become a personalized searchable food library.

US-004

Hormone and Skin Check-In

As a user, I want to track hormone-related symptoms, cycle context, acne, and stress so that I can notice patterns over time.

  • Users can log cycle day or phase, acne severity or location, mood, energy, cravings, digestion, bloating, sleep, and stress.
  • Users can add custom symptoms and free-text observations.
  • Trends use cautious language and do not imply diagnosis or guaranteed cause.

US-005

Naturopathic Health Consult

As a user, I want a consult-like section that asks holistic questions and suggests areas to consider so that I can reflect on my health more deeply.

  • Consult flows ask structured questions about digestion, stress, sleep, skin, cycle, food, supplements, hydration, and lifestyle.
  • AI-generated responses are framed as educational considerations, not personalized diagnosis or treatment.
  • The consult can reference the user's logged patterns when consent is granted.

US-006

Supplements and Nutrient Guidance

As a user, I want the Supplements tab to suggest possible vitamin, mineral, and supplement areas to consider based on my goals, symptoms, acne patterns, and nutrition data so that I know what to discuss or investigate next.

  • Users can log feelings and symptoms that may relate to nutrition status, such as fatigue, dizziness, low mood, cravings, poor sleep, brittle nails, or heavy cycles.
  • The app can suggest possible nutrient areas to consider based on user goals such as acne support or protein needs, without diagnosing a deficiency.
  • Suggestions include plain-language rationale, relevant user inputs, and guidance to consider labs or a qualified professional when appropriate.
  • Suggestions can include supplement ideas, clinician questions, and nutrient areas to investigate, with clear safety boundaries.
  • Users can track supplements with name, dosage notes, frequency, timing, reason for taking, and adherence state.
  • The app includes safety copy about supplement interactions, pregnancy, medications, medical conditions, and professional review.

US-006A

Acne Flare Photo Consult

As a user, I want to photograph an acne flare and receive holistic follow-up questions and wellness suggestions so that the app can help me think through inside-out care patterns.

  • Users can take or upload an acne flare photo for Gemini-supported visual review with explicit consent.
  • The app does not store acne images by default after analysis.
  • The app may store the assessment summary, user answers, and non-drug wellness suggestions for future comparison and follow-up recommendations.
  • Gemini asks clarifying questions before generating suggestions when the initial image and logged context are insufficient.
  • All output is framed as wellness education and cannot claim to diagnose, treat, or cure acne.

US-007

Lifestyle Stress and Anxiety Support

As a user, I want lifestyle suggestions for reducing stress and anxiety so that I can support hormone health and daily resilience.

  • The Lifestyle tab recommends practices such as breathwork, meditation, journaling, gentle movement, gratitude, grounding, sleep wind-down, and screen-boundary rituals.
  • Users can save, complete, skip, and rate practices.
  • Recommendations adapt to stated goals and tracked stress, anxiety, sleep, mood, exercise, and cycle context.

US-008

AI Settings, API Key, and Model Selection

As a user, I want to add my Gemini API key and choose the Gemini model powering AI features so that the app can use my configured AI access responsibly.

  • Settings include a secure Gemini API key entry field with paste, show/hide, save, and clear actions.
  • Settings include a model selector exposed to the user because the user supplies their own API key.
  • The app stores the user's model preference and applies it to consult and recommendation features.
  • The UI explains that model availability, cost, token usage, speed, and capabilities may vary.
  • Settings include API cost tracking for daily, weekly, average, and total estimated cost when usage data is available.

US-009

Health Data Trust Controls

As a user, I want clear controls over my health data so that I understand what is stored and can manage it.

  • Users can export and delete personal health data.
  • Privacy settings explain storage, AI use, sharing, and any future integrations in plain language.
  • Consent is explicit for notifications, sensitive categories, AI consult context, and any future provider sharing.

US-010

Macro and Health Visualizations

As a user, I want visual summaries of my macros and health patterns so that I can understand trends without focusing on calories.

  • Dashboard visualizations show protein, carbohydrate, and fat totals over daily and weekly periods.
  • Charts can compare macro patterns with selected hormone, skin, stress, supplement, or mindfulness signals.
  • Visualizations exclude calorie deficit, calorie burn, and weight-loss countdown framing.

US-011

Post-MVP Meal Planning

As a user, I may later want saved meal ideas and planned meals, but this should not be part of the first MVP.

  • MVP excludes saved meal ideas, dated planned meals, AI-assisted meal plans, recipe builder, and full weekly meal plans.
  • The food module focuses on logging what the user actually ate and growing the custom JSON food library.
  • Meal planning can be revisited after the food logging and health assessment workflows are useful.

US-012

Exercise by Intensity and Duration

As a user, I want to log weightlifting and sprint-burst exercise by selecting intensity and duration so that tracking is fast and does not require entering individual exercises.

  • The Exercise tab supports weightlifting with low, medium, and high intensity choices plus duration in minutes.
  • The Exercise tab supports sprint bursts with activity choices such as running, stationary bike, rowing, and boxing, plus low, medium, and high intensity and duration in minutes.
  • Duration is adjustable with a slider or equivalent iOS control from 5 to 60 minutes in 5-minute increments, with selected minutes visible before saving.
  • Users can save multiple exercise sessions per day and see a simple daily exercise summary.
  • The app does not require selecting or entering individual lifts, sets, reps, or exercise names in MVP.

US-013

Saved Food Portion Sizing

As a user, I want to choose a saved food from my JSON macro library and adjust the portion size so that logging common foods is fast and accurate enough for macro trend analysis.

  • Saved-food search returns matching JSON library records with serving description, calories, protein, carbohydrate, and fat values.
  • The selected food screen shows default amount chips for 25%, 50%, 75%, exact amount, 150%, and double, plus a custom amount input.
  • Changing the multiplier recalculates calories, protein, carbohydrates, and fat before the user saves.
  • Users can cancel or log the food immediately. Planning actions are deferred out of MVP.
  • The flow does not show deficit, burn, or weight-loss calculations.

US-015

Visualize Food From Menu

As a user, I want to photograph a menu or paste dish text and have the app visualize the food before calculating macros so that I can estimate protein, carbs, and fat when no food photo exists.

  • Users can take a menu photo, upload a menu screenshot, or type/paste a dish description.
  • Gemini generates a food visualization based on the menu item or dish description.
  • The app can then use the generated food image and text context to estimate serving size, calories, protein, carbohydrates, and fat.
  • The user must confirm or edit the generated macro estimate before logging the food or saving it to the JSON library.
  • The feature is not a restaurant recommendation or Better Choices flow.

US-014

Manual Health Assessment

As a user, I want to manually create a health assessment whenever I choose so that I can review macros, eating patterns, treatments, holistic practices, and acne-photo findings on demand.

  • The assessment is manually initiated by the user and can be run at whatever frequency the user wants.
  • The assessment can include macros, eating patterns, active treatments, holistic treatments, acne logs, acne photo findings, cycle context, symptoms, supplements, lifestyle practices, exercise, and consult responses.
  • Users can review which data categories are included before generating the report.
  • The generated report includes pattern summary, possible contributors, next focus areas, holistic options to consider, and cited references when specific sources are available.
  • The report is editable and savable as a health assessment record.
  • The report avoids diagnosis, treatment planning, supplement dosing claims, and calorie/weight-loss framing.

Functional Requirements

  1. FR-1: The system must let users create a women's health profile with hormone, skin, cycle, nutrition, supplement, stress, sleep, and wellness goals.
  2. FR-2: The system must let users log daily meals with food name, meal category, serving notes, calories as optional secondary data, protein, carbohydrates, fat, and optional notes.
  3. FR-3: The system must calculate daily totals for protein, carbohydrates, fat, and secondary calories, with protein visually emphasized. MVP must track macros without requiring macro targets.
  4. FR-4: The system must not present itself as a calorie tracker or weight-loss app; calories may be retained as secondary food data, but calorie deficit, burn rate, and weight-loss strategy must not be core UI concepts.
  5. FR-5: The system must maintain a user-specific, editable JSON-backed food macro library of confirmed foods, meals, serving descriptions, calories, protein, carbohydrates, fat, source, confirmation date, and last-used date.
  6. FR-6: When a user types what they ate, the system must search the local food macro library before calling Gemini for a new macro estimate. A saved food match should be based on the primary food item, such as chicken, cheeseburger, or another canonical item name.
  7. FR-7: When Gemini estimates macros for a new food or meal, the system must require user confirmation or editing before saving the item to the daily log or JSON-backed library. Confirmed entries must become searchable reusable records for future logging.
  8. FR-8: The system must let users photograph or upload meal photos and request Gemini image analysis for food identification and macro estimation, including multi-item meals and restaurant plates.
  9. FR-9: Photo-based macro results must be editable and must only be added to the daily log and saved food macro library after user confirmation.
  10. FR-10: The system must let users track hormone and skin signals, including cycle phase or day, acne severity or location, mood, energy, stress, digestion, bloating, cravings, sleep, and custom symptoms.
  11. FR-11: The system must provide a naturopathic-style consult section that asks adaptive questions one at a time, up to 10 questions, and generates educational wellness considerations and a practitioner-style report with cited references where available.
  12. FR-12: The system must organize the primary mobile interface around four bottom tabs: Lifestyle, Supplements, Macro, and Exercise.
  13. FR-13: The Lifestyle tab must recommend daily lifestyle, mindfulness, meditative, and therapeutic practices for reducing stress, reducing anxiety, slowing down, and supporting nervous system regulation. Recommendations must react to user goals, logged data, and consult responses.
  14. FR-14: The Supplements tab must let users log feelings, symptoms, goals, supplement routines, and nutrition signals that may relate to vitamin, mineral, protein, or supplement needs.
  15. FR-15: The Supplements tab must suggest possible nutrient or supplement areas to consider based on user goals and inputs, while clearly stating that these are not diagnoses and may require labs or professional review. Suggestions may include supplement ideas, clinician questions, and areas to investigate.
  16. FR-16: The system must let users track supplements with name, dosage notes, schedule, adherence status, reason for taking, start date, pause or discontinued state, and free-text notes.
  17. FR-17: The Exercise tab must let users log weightlifting by selecting low, medium, or high intensity and duration rather than entering individual exercises.
  18. FR-18: The Exercise tab must let users log sprint-burst sessions by selecting an activity such as running, bike, rowing, or boxing, plus low, medium, or high intensity and duration rather than entering individual sprint drills.

Non-Goals

  • Diagnosis, treatment planning, emergency response, prescription advice, or claims that the app cures acne, hormone disorders, or other medical conditions.
  • Replacement for physicians, dermatologists, endocrinologists, gynecologists, registered dietitians, naturopathic doctors, therapists, or other qualified professionals.
  • Automatic wearable, EHR, pharmacy, lab, or food database integrations in the initial version unless later product work makes one integration essential.
  • Provider billing, insurance workflows, or regulated clinical documentation in the initial version.
  • Public social networking, leaderboards, or guilt-driven streak mechanics.
  • Calorie-tracker positioning, calorie-deficit optimization, rigid calorie restriction, diet culture framing, acne shame, or fear-based supplement recommendations. Calories may exist only as secondary food metadata.
  • Diagnosing vitamin or mineral deficiencies from symptoms alone, replacing lab work, or recommending high-risk supplement dosing without professional review.
  • Detailed exercise programming, exercise libraries, individual lift tracking, sets, reps, progressive overload planning, or athletic coaching in MVP.
  • Copying code, assets, calorie-deficit logic, branding, design language, or product positioning from the reference Health-Tracker repository. The repository is inspiration for technical patterns only.

UX and Design Considerations

  • Primary navigation should use four bottom tabs: Lifestyle, Supplements, Macro, and Exercise.
  • Settings should be reachable from a top-right gear/profile control rather than competing with the four primary bottom tabs.
  • The Lifestyle tab should prioritize immediate stress and anxiety support: today's suggested practice, saved practices, completion history, and related symptom context.
  • The Supplements tab should prioritize careful supplement and nutrient guidance: user goals, feelings, possible nutrient areas to consider, supplement tracking, clinician questions, and safety notes.
  • The Macro tab should contain the food data module: Add Food, New Food, Log Meal, Food Log, Daily Totals, Visualize Food, editable JSON library reuse, multi-item photo/upload macro analysis, and macro totals. Meal planning is deferred out of MVP.
  • The detailed food log should follow a practical capture structure: search saved foods, add a new food, describe a meal, attach a camera/photo-library image, review the itemized log, and see protein-forward daily totals with carbs, fat, and secondary calories.
  • Saved-food selection should make portion sizing obvious with amount chips, a custom amount field, and a visible recalculated calories/protein/carbs/fat result before the user taps Log.
  • The Exercise tab should be fast: select exercise type, select activity when relevant, select low/medium/high intensity, set duration, save session, and review daily summary.
  • Exercise duration should feel like the reference slider interaction, using 5 to 60 minutes in 5-minute increments, but the app should omit calorie-burn and deficit estimates.
  • The acne photo UI should feel like a primary app workflow, not a buried support feature, because inside-out acne care is the top product priority.
  • Health assessment creation should be manually initiated and should let users choose included data categories before generating an editable wellness report.
  • Language should be calm, specific, and non-judgmental, especially around food, body, acne, adherence, and missed routines.
  • Macro tracking should be fast enough for repeated daily use, reuse known foods whenever possible, and avoid making users feel punished for incomplete logs.
  • Macro confirmation screens should clearly show food item, portion assumption, calories as secondary data, protein, carbohydrates, and fat before the user saves results from text, photo, or Visualize Food analysis.
  • Acne flare review should be explicitly consent-based, clear that photos are not stored by default, and focused on holistic non-drug wellness suggestions rather than treatment claims.
  • Consult and insight screens should be visually scannable and careful about medical interpretation.
  • The visual direction should follow the provided Ristoro design guide: aged paper surfaces, midnight navy ink, antique brass/gold, botanical verde, restrained rosso accents, sharp Art Deco frames, seal-like controls, poster-style uppercase labels, and calm Italian wellness language used sparingly.
  • Accessibility must include sufficient contrast, Dynamic Type support, VoiceOver-friendly labels, visible focus states where relevant, and touch targets appropriate for iPhone.

Tab Data Model Notes
  • Lifestyle records should include recommendation type, trigger context, completion state, user rating, and notes.
  • Supplement guidance records should include reported feelings, user goals, possible nutrient area, rationale, confidence, safety notes, suggested next step, linked supplement records, and whether the user marked the suggestion as relevant.
  • Macro records should remain macro-centered for product positioning: protein, carbohydrate, and fat are the primary nutrition fields, with calories allowed as secondary food metadata.
  • Acne flare assessment records should include user consent timestamp, image analysis summary, follow-up question responses, non-drug wellness suggestions, references when available, and should not include the source image by default.
  • Exercise records should include exercise category, intensity, duration, timestamp, optional notes, and source. MVP categories are weightlifting and sprint burst.
Macro Library and Gemini Efficiency
  • The macro tracker should use a local JSON-backed food library as the first lookup layer for typed meal descriptions and previously confirmed photo results.
  • Suggested JSON record fields include id, canonicalName, aliases, servingDescription, defaultServingQuantity, defaultServingUnit, calories, proteinGrams, carbohydrateGrams, fatGrams, source, confidence, userConfirmed, createdAt, updatedAt, lastUsedAt, and notes.
  • Daily meal log records should store the library item reference when available, selected serving amount, custom serving notes, timestamp, meal type, calories, protein grams, carbohydrate grams, fat grams, and source path such as manual, library, typed Gemini, photo Gemini, or visualized-menu Gemini.
  • The JSON macro library should be editable, locally stored, optionally synced through iCloud, and importable from a saved JSON database.
  • Gemini should only be called when the app cannot confidently match the user's typed entry or selected photo result to an existing confirmed library item. Matching should prioritize the primary food item or canonical meal name.
  • Confirmed Gemini results should become reusable library records, which improves speed, lowers AI usage, avoids repeat image analysis for common foods, and supports future personal pattern analysis.
  • The app should keep daily meal logs separate from the reusable macro library so users can edit one logged meal without corrupting the saved reference food.
AI Model Requirements
  • The app must never hardcode a Gemini API key. MVP should support a Settings-entered user API key stored securely in iOS Keychain; a backend proxy can be considered later if account-based service billing is needed.
  • Supported model choices for PRD planning are Gemini 2.5 Flash and Gemini 3.5 Flash, with room to add Gemini Pro-class models for acne flare photo review if the user provides access through their own API key.
  • The app should include fallback behavior when a selected model is unavailable, rate-limited, too costly, or blocked by policy.
  • Food text parsing should request structured JSON output with foods, portions, calories, protein grams, carbohydrate grams, fat grams, confidence, assumptions, and source notes.
  • Photo and Visualize Food analysis should request structured JSON output and must surface assumptions to the user before saving.
  • Settings should show whether an API key is configured and expose token, request, and estimated cost history when Gemini returns usage metadata. Cost summaries should include daily, weekly, average, and total cost.
  • Prompts must constrain AI output to wellness education, reflection, habit support, and questions for qualified professionals. Prompts must forbid diagnosis, treatment plans, medication changes, or urgent-care triage.
  • Health assessment prompts should request structured JSON output with included data sources, pattern summary, evidence caveats, current focus areas, active treatments, holistic treatments, acne photo findings, and references when specific sources are available.
Regulatory and Safety Notes
  • Current direction is a custom one-person wellness and education app with functional medicine and naturopathic-style framing, not a clinical product.
  • PRD should explicitly avoid FDA-style treatment claims unless the product is intentionally pursuing clinical validation.
  • Privacy posture can start with local phone storage and optional iCloud sync because this is a one-person app, but data export and deletion should still be available.
  • Supplement guidance should avoid drug/medication changes and high-risk dosing claims. Heavy safety infrastructure or a safety cofounder is not required for the custom first version.

Success Metrics

  1. Activation: percentage of users who complete onboarding, set a health goal, and log the first meal or check-in.
  2. Macro tracking consistency: percentage of users who log macro entries on at least four days in week two.
  3. Macro usefulness: percentage of users who view daily macro totals, especially protein visibility, and report that the summary helps them make food decisions.
  4. Macro library efficiency: percentage of meal logs resolved from the user's saved macro library without a Gemini call.
  5. Meal photo capture adoption: percentage of active users who successfully save at least one photo-confirmed multi-item meal macro entry.
  6. Saved food efficiency: percentage of common food logs completed through saved JSON records and serving amount presets without a Gemini call.
  7. Acne consult adoption: percentage of active users who complete an acne flare photo review, answer follow-up questions, and save an assessment summary.
  8. Manual assessment adoption: number of manually initiated health assessments generated and saved after meaningful logged inputs.
  9. AI settings readiness: percentage of users who configure a Gemini key, choose a model, view estimated usage cost, and successfully complete at least one AI-assisted macro parse or photo analysis.
  10. Lifestyle adoption: percentage of users who complete at least two stress or anxiety support practices per week.
  11. Supplement relevance: percentage of users who mark a supplement or nutrient-area suggestion as relevant or worth discussing with a professional.
  12. Exercise simplicity: percentage of exercise sessions logged in under 30 seconds using intensity and duration.
  13. Supplement adherence: percentage of users who create a supplement routine and mark adherence at least three days per week.
  14. Insight value: percentage of users who report clearer understanding of food, stress, cycle, supplements, acne, or hormone patterns.
  15. Trust: percentage of users who understand privacy controls, AI consent, and wellness-only boundaries without confusion.
